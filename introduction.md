# Introduction – Threat Detection Report 2022 (Analysis & Notes)

## Purpose of the Report
Red Canary analyzed more than **30,000 confirmed threats** across customer environments to identify:
- The most common adversary techniques (MITRE ATT&CK)
- The most prevalent threats and malware families
- Key trends shaping the threat landscape
- Actionable detection and mitigation guidance

The goal of the report is to help defenders:
- Understand real-world adversary behavior
- Improve detection engineering
- Strengthen incident response
- Prioritize security controls based on actual threat data

---

## How to Use This Report
- Review the most common techniques and threats observed in real environments.
- Learn how to detect, mitigate, and simulate each threat.
- Map the insights to your own security controls and SOC strategy.
- Use the trends to anticipate how adversaries are evolving.

---

## Red Canary SOC Functions
Red Canary’s security operations team consists of three major components:

### 1. Intelligence Team
- Identifies and analyzes distinct threats
- Tracks malware families, threat groups, and activity clusters

### 2. Detection Engineering & Enablement
- Builds and maintains ~3,000 detection analytics
- Maps analytics to MITRE ATT&CK techniques/sub-techniques
- Ensures reliable and high-fidelity detections

### 3. Incident Handling Team
- Responds to confirmed threats
- Contains intrusions before impact
- Provides remediation guidance

---

## Key Takeaways
- The report is based on **real confirmed threats**, not theoretical data.
- It focuses on **behavioral detection**, not signatures.
- It highlights **emerging trends** that defenders must adapt to.
- It provides **actionable detection logic** useful for SOC analysts and BTLO labs.
